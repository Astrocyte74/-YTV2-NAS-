📝 Key Insights Summary:
The Saucony Endorphin Azura is a highly anticipated non-plated training shoe designed to bridge the gap between tempo specialists and long-distance grinders. Positioned as a versatile alternative to shoes like the Adidas Evo SL and Asics Super Blast, it offers a stable, responsive ride suitable for a variety of training paces.

**Introduction to Saucony Endorphin Azura**
*   The shoe is a new addition to the Endorphin lineup, serving as a dedicated non-plated trainer.
*   It acts as a strategic replacement for the Endorphin Trainer and the Kinvara Pro.
*   The video positions it as a "hybrid" competitor that sits between the price and performance of the Evo SL and the Super Blast.

**Technical Stats and Features**
*   The midsole features a full-length Power Run PB foam, the same material used in the Endorphin Speed series.
*   It has a stack height of 40mm in the heel and 32mm in the forefoot, resulting in an 8mm drop.
*   The shoe weighs 255g in a UK size 9.5 and utilizes Saucony's signature Speedroll technology.
*   The outsole consists of XT900 rubber, though the reviewer found its "strategic" placement and wet-weather grip to be underwhelming.

**Upper Design and Build Quality**
*   The upper is constructed from a high-quality mesh with overlays that provide structure and support.
*   The heel area features significant padding for comfort and a unique heel clip for added stability.
*   The design includes a wide platform and a deep toe box that fits true to size without causing rubbing.

**Ride Experience and Performance**
*   Testing over 20+ miles revealed a ride that is firmer than expected, reminiscent of the original Endorphin Speed.
*   The aggressive toe spring and Speedroll technology provide a noticeable forward-rolling sensation during transitions.
*   The shoe handles paces ranging from 8:30 per mile down to roughly 7:00 per mile effectively.

**Versatility and Use Cases**
*   The shoe is highly versatile, capable of handling everything from 10k runs to long-distance half-marathon training.
*   Its firm foam and wide base make it an excellent stable option for heavier runners who find other super-trainers too unstable.
*   While the Evo SL may be faster for short treadmill bursts, the Azura is preferred for longer efforts beyond 10 miles.

**Pricing and Value**
*   The retail price is set at £140, making it an attractive mid-range option.
*   Discounts of 15% are available for running club members through specific partners, and 10% codes can be found on the Fordy Runs website.
*   The reviewer considers the value high because the shoe excels in multiple roles that usually require two different pairs of shoes.

Bottom line: The Saucony Endorphin Azura is a highly versatile, stable, and well-priced daily trainer that lives up to its hype despite some traction issues in the rain.
